Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources like banks or online services.

In a typical phishing attack, the user receives a message with a link. When they click it, they are taken to a fake website that looks real. The user enters their login details, which are then stolen by the attacker.

Students and new internet users are often targeted because they may not recognize such scams. The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

Awareness and careful verification of links and messages can help prevent such attacks.
